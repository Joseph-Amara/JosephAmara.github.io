Measuring Urban Nature Accessibility

Visualising the 3-30-300 Guideline for Amsterdam West

An interactive geospatial analysis of Amsterdam West's compliance with the 3-30-300 urban greening guideline. The project combines visibility analysis, satellite-derived vegetation metrics and network accessibility modelling to evaluate how effectively neighbourhoods provide access to urban nature.

Leaflet VS Code Python Sentinel-2 OpenStreetMap Amsterdam Tree Dataset
Objective
Create an interactive visualisation showing which buildings meet one or more of the 3-30-300 criteria and identify areas where urban greening efforts could be prioritised.
Method
Python workflows were used to analyse tree visibility, neighbourhood green coverage and walking accessibility to green spaces. The outputs were combined into an interactive Leaflet web map that visualises individual and combined performance across Amsterdam West.
Outcome
An interactive map showing which buildings meet the 3-tree, 30% canopy and 300-metre green space criteria, either individually or in combination.

Mapping Environmental Inequality

Houston's Heat Divide

This project explores how urban heat exposure varies across Harris County, Texas. By combining satellite imagery with socio-economic data, the study examines the links between vegetation cover, surface temperature and income levels across different neighbourhoods.

ArcGIS StoryMaps ArcGIS Pro Landsat US Census Data NDVI Land Surface Temperature
Objective
Understand how heat exposure differs between neighbourhoods and provide evidence that can support more equitable climate adaptation planning.
Method
Land Surface Temperature and NDVI were calculated from Landsat imagery and combined with income data from the US Census Bureau. Spatial analysis was then used to identify patterns between wealth, vegetation cover and heat exposure.
Outcome
The analysis showed that wealthier neighbourhoods generally contained more green space and experienced lower temperatures than less affluent areas. Results were presented through an interactive StoryMap.

Finding the Optimal Route

Proposed Industrial Wastewater Pipeline Route in the Maas-Waal Region

A spatial suitability and routing analysis designed to identify the most suitable path for a new industrial wastewater pipeline while minimising environmental, topographic and land-use constraints.

ArcGIS Pro ModelBuilder AHN Elevation Data Land Use Data Cost Distance Least Cost Path
Objective
Identify the most suitable route for a new wastewater pipeline while meeting environmental and planning constraints across the study area.
Method
A complete routing workflow was developed in ArcGIS ModelBuilder. Spatial overlays, suitability analysis, cost-distance modelling and least-cost path tools were combined into a repeatable geoprocessing model.
Outcome
A reproducible routing model capable of identifying an efficient and compliant pipeline route while considering multiple geographic constraints.
Machine Learning for Rapid Flood Detection

Comparing Global Thresholding and Random Forest Classification for Flood Mapping

This project compares a traditional threshold-based approach with a Random Forest model for flood mapping using Sentinel-1 radar imagery. The 2023 Kakhovka Dam breach was used as a real-world case study to evaluate which approach produced more reliable flood extent maps.

Google Earth Engine ArcGIS Pro Sentinel-1 Random Forest Classification
Objective
Assess whether machine learning can improve flood mapping accuracy compared with a traditional global thresholding approach.
Method
Pre-flood and flood-period Sentinel-1 images were processed and classified using both Random Forest and Global Thresholding techniques. Results were then compared against a reference dataset.
Outcome
The Random Forest approach produced more accurate flood maps and reduced classification errors across complex landscapes.

Designing Climate-Resilient Neighbourhoods

From Grey to Green: A Climate-Adaptive Redesign of De Buurt, Wageningen

A redesign proposal for a neighbourhood in Wageningen aimed at reducing heat stress, improving biodiversity and increasing access to green space through nature-based solutions and sustainable urban design.

ArcGIS Pro QGIS Canva Sentinel-2 Multi-Criteria Analysis
Objective
Improve climate resilience and environmental quality within the neighbourhood while maintaining usability for local residents.
Method
GIS-based Multi-Criteria Analysis was used to identify priority intervention areas. Green corridors, bioswales, wildflower meadows and other nature-based solutions were then incorporated into a proposed redesign.
Outcome
A practical redesign strategy that improves biodiversity, cooling capacity and access to green infrastructure while creating a more attractive public space.
